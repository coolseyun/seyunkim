Hello! I'm Seyun Kim. I'm a first year Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University School of Computer Science Human-Computer Interaction Institute (HCII) advised by Haiyi Zhu and Motahhare Eslami .

Previously, I received my Master of Science (M.S.) in Computer Science at Cornell University . I was advised by Malte Jung in the Robots in Groups Lab . I received my B.S. at Cornell University majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Information Science.

My research focuses on how we can make technologies accountable and transparent by understanding the impact of technologies on users and affected communities. I am interested in enhancing transparency and mitigating bias in algorithmic systems. My current project is understanding fairness critiera in public sector algorithms.
